Validating your data

OpenActive have developed a validation tool to allow you to check that your JSON data feeds conform to the latest specification. Read on to see how to leverage this tool to assess your conformance.

Navigate to the validation tool at https://validator.openactive.io/.

The validation console

Once there, you will see a screen divided into two panels. The left panel allows you to enter data for validation, the right panel will show you the results of the validation.

Pasting in JSON

The left-hand panel accepts the JSON content that you would like to validate. You can paste JSON strings into the editor on the left, and click Validate to run the validation.

This JSON can take one of two forms:

An RPDE feed

The validator will perform limited validation on an RPDE feed.

It will inspect the structure of the feed, and report on any missed fields.

It will also take the first 10 non-deleted items in the feed, and validate the data property against the Modelling Opportunity Data Specification.

A JSON data model

This is a fragment of JSON that conforms to one of the models outlined in the models section. It will typically be an Event model, which will form the data portion of an RPDE feed item.

The validator will check whether this fragment conforms to the Modelling Opportunity Data Specification.

Validating a URL

You can also ask the validator to validate a URL.

To do this:

  • Click Load URL in the top bar of the page
  • Enter your URL into the text box
  • Click Open

Interpreting the results

After clicking Validate, the validator will return a list of notices, errors, warnings and tips.

  • Notices will notify you about any restrictions or limitationsthe validator has encountered whilst processing your data.
  • Errors will notify you about any issues with your data that MUST be fixed.
  • Warnings will notify you about any issues with your data that SHOULD be fixed.
  • Tips will notify you about how data consumers may perceive your data, or how you could improve your data quality.

Clicking on a result will jump the editor on the left to the line and column that the result pertains to.

Managing the results

The validator affords lots of options for the filtering and sorting of the results.

Grouping the results

By default, similar notices will be grouped together.

To change this behaviour, simply:

  • Click Filters.
  • Uncheck the Group messages option.
Filtering the results

You can show or hide different severities or categories by:

  • Click Filters.
  • Check or uncheck the option you'd like to show or hide.
Sorting the results

You can sort the results either by line number or severity:

  • Click Sort.
  • Check the sort option you desire.
